# Interpreting Market Health

## What Market Health Tells You

Market health combines multiple indicators to assess the overall investment environment. A healthy market means easier exits and more predictable outcomes.

## Market Health Indicators

### Key Metrics Summary

| Indicator | Healthy | Warning | Concerning |
|-----------|---------|---------|------------|
| Price Trend | +3-10% YoY | 0-3% | Negative |
| DOM | < 45 days | 45-90 days | > 90 days |
| Inventory | < 5 months | 5-7 months | > 7 months |
| Sale-to-List | > 95% | 90-95% | < 90% |
| Population | Growing | Stable | Declining |
| Employment | Growing | Stable | Declining |

### Reading a Health Report
```
Market: Miami, FL 33139

Overall Health Score: 78/100 (Healthy)

Metrics:
- Price Trend: +7.2% YoY (Strong)
- DOM: 28 days (Fast)
- Inventory: 3.2 months (Seller's market)
- Sale-to-List: 98% (Strong demand)
- Population Growth: +1.8% (Growing)
- Employment: +2.5% (Strong)

Assessment: Strong, healthy market favorable for wholesale
```

## Health Score Interpretation

| Score | Health Level | Strategy |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| 80-100 | Very Healthy | Confident investment, standard MAO |
| 65-79 | Healthy | Good market, normal approach |
| 50-64 | Moderate | Careful selection, conservative ARV |
| 35-49 | Weak | Selective deals, larger margins |
| < 35 | Distressed | Avoid or require exceptional deals |

## Supply/Demand Balance

### Seller's Market (< 4 months inventory)
**Characteristics:**
- Multiple offers common
- Quick sales
- Prices rising
- Low negotiating room

**Wholesale Strategy:**
- Act fast on opportunities
- Buyers are eager
- Can price closer to ARV
- Competition for deals

### Balanced Market (4-6 months)
**Characteristics:**
- Stable conditions
- Normal sales pace
- Prices stable or slight growth
- Standard negotiations

**Wholesale Strategy:**
- Standard MAO formulas work
- Normal timeline expectations
- Multiple exit strategies viable

### Buyer's Market (> 6 months)
**Characteristics:**
- Excess inventory
- Longer sale times
- Flat or declining prices
- More negotiating room

**Wholesale Strategy:**
- Require larger discounts
- Pre-identify buyers
- Conservative ARV
- Longer exit timelines

## Economic Health Factors

### Employment Health
| Indicator | Healthy | Concerning |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Unemployment rate | < 5% | > 7% |
| Job growth | Positive | Negative |
| Major employers | Diversified | Concentrated |
| New business | Growing | Declining |

### Population Health
| Indicator | Healthy | Concerning |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Population growth | > 1%/year | Declining |
| Net migration | Positive | Negative |
| Age distribution | Balanced | Aging rapidly |
| Household formation | Growing | Declining |

### Affordability Health
| Indicator | Healthy | Concerning |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Price-to-income ratio | < 5x | > 7x |
| Rent-to-income | < 30% | > 40% |
| Mortgage affordability | Manageable | Stretched |

## Trend Analysis

### Improving Market Signs
- DOM decreasing
- Inventory declining
- Prices rising steadily
- Population growing
- Employment strengthening
- New construction increasing

### Declining Market Signs
- DOM increasing
- Inventory building
- Price growth slowing/negative
- Population flat/declining
- Employment weakening
- Construction slowing

### Stable Market Signs
- Consistent metrics over time
- Normal seasonal variations
- No extreme movements
- Balanced supply/demand

## Risk Assessment by Health

### Low Risk (Healthy Market)
- Multiple exit strategies
- Quick buyer placement
- ARV holds during hold
- Standard contingencies sufficient

### Moderate Risk (Moderate Health)
- Pre-identify buyer preferred
- Conservative ARV
- Longer timeline buffer
- Multiple backup plans

### High Risk (Weak/Distressed)
- Must have buyer first
- Very conservative ARV
- Large margin required
- Rental exit backup

## Market Cycle Position

### Expansion Phase
**Signs:**
- Rising prices
- Falling inventory
- Increasing construction
- Strong employment

**Strategy:** More aggressive, faster pace

### Peak Phase
**Signs:**
- Rapid price increases
- Very low inventory
- Construction boom
- Affordability stretched

**Strategy:** Caution, don't chase, be selective

### Contraction Phase
**Signs:**
- Price growth slowing
- Inventory rising
- Construction slowing
- Employment softening

**Strategy:** Defensive, larger margins, be patient

### Trough Phase
**Signs:**
- Prices stabilizing after decline
- Inventory stabilizing
- Very low construction
- Employment bottoming

**Strategy:** Opportunity, be ready for recovery

## Health by Property Type

Different segments may have different health:

| Segment | May Be Healthy | May Be Weak |
|---------|----------------|-------------|
| Entry-level | Strong demand | Price ceiling |
| Mid-market | Broad demand | Most competitive |
| Luxury | Strong buyers | Limited pool |
| Multi-family | Strong rental demand | Rate sensitive |
| Condos | First-time buyers | HOA concerns |

**Action:** Check segment-specific health, not just overall market.

## Using Health for Decisions

### Market Entry Decision
```
Health Score > 65: Good market to enter
Health Score 50-65: Enter selectively
Health Score < 50: Wait or find better market
```

### MAO Adjustment
```
Very Healthy (80+): Standard 70% rule
Healthy (65-79): Use 70% rule
Moderate (50-64): Use 65-68%
Weak (35-49): Use 60-65%
Distressed (<35): Use 55-60% or avoid
```

### Resource Allocation
Focus more effort on healthier markets:
- Marketing spend
- Time allocation
- Buyer network building
- Deal volume targets
